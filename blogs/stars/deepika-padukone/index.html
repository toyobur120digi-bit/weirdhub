Deepika Padukone and the Strange Superstitions She Still Follows

Even global icons have their quirks. Deepika Padukone has spoken openly about the unusual rituals and beliefs she still holds onto from her early days.

The Pre-Fame Rituals

Long before she became the highest-paid actress in India and a global brand ambassador, Deepika was a regular girl from Bangalore with big dreams and some very specific superstitions.

One of her most well-known quirks is her habit of touching her father’s feet before leaving for any important shoot or event. What started as a simple sign of respect slowly turned into a non-negotiable ritual that she still practices today — even when her father isn’t physically present.

She has also admitted to carrying a small silver coin in her purse for years because her grandmother told her it would bring good luck. The coin has traveled with her to film sets across the world.

The “No New Clothes” Rule

One of the strangest habits Deepika has openly discussed is her reluctance to wear brand new clothes for the first day of shooting.

She prefers to wear something she has already worn at least once — a practice rooted in an old family belief that new clothes can bring “new problems.” While she laughs about it now, she admits she still follows this rule for major projects.

Her team has learned to work around this particular superstition. They now prepare “pre-worn” versions of outfits specifically for the first day of principal photography.

Why These Weird Habits Matter

In an industry full of pressure and uncertainty, these small rituals give Deepika a sense of control and grounding. They connect her to her roots and family even when she’s living a life that looks nothing like the one she grew up with.

She has said in interviews that these habits are not about fear — they’re about comfort and continuity. In a world that changes at lightning speed, holding onto a few “weird” traditions can be deeply humanizing.

Whether it’s the silver coin, the feet-touching ritual, or the pre-worn clothes rule, these small acts reveal a side of Deepika Padukone that millions of fans rarely get to see: a superstar who is still, in many ways, that girl from Bangalore.
